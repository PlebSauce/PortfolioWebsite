A Story About Ambition

Who I Was

My journey here so far and development

During the pandemic, many people felt discouraged and saw all the negatives that came with it. However, I decided that I couldn't sit complacently. So I swapped from public school to homeschooling so that I could do two things. One take 63 units, an associates worth, of college credits through community college. Two, pursue the beginning of my career in computer science on my own terms. With all this extra time, I took a deep dive into everything I could get my hands on. I didn't let this obstacle stop me, I used it as an opprotunity for growth instead.

Yet again when I eventually graduated highschool and went to a four year university, I found that I was faced with another brick wall. I was not able to get classes I needed in a reasonable amount of time, added at least one or two extra semesters. Plus these classes didn't seem to be teaching me what I needed to learn. So I swapped to Western Governors University where I worked for hours upon hours every day to pursue my degree in Computer Science.

Who I Am

Current projects and what I do

Upon graduating, I knew that there was still many more skills I could pick up. The more I learned the more I realized how little I knew. I now spend my days trying to learn as much as I can about anything and everything that is relevant and/or interests me. I try to cover a wide range of projects such as various web pages that use many technologies such as node.js, angular, springboot, mySQL, pgadmin4, and many other still to come. I also have gone the complete opposite direction and have begun working on my own interpreter that I will continue to grow and expand. My github is littered with all sorts of projects with many more on the way.

Who I Will Be

My goals and aspirations for the future

I know that I can be a great asset to any team I am a part of. I'd like to become part of a group that produces quality work in a timely manner. Working with many people from various backgrounds and skill levels is the best way to learn, and that learning process is something I'd like to be a part of. Aside from that, I'd also like to learn more and get better in a variety of subjects such as express.js, GoLang, multithreaded software solutions, and website security practices.
